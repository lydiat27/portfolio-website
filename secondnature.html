Second Nature
Where sustainability should be Second Nature
CONTEXT
FLOW Designathon
ROLE
Researcher, Designer
TOOLS USED
Figma
Problem
How might we encourage sustainable community practices: Barriers and stigma involved in using second hand products
Research
  • Thrift store prices have risen nearly 30% from 2020 - 2024
  • 92 million tonnes of clothes end up in landfills
  • The average Canadian household contributes 140 kilograms of yearly food waste, with Canada wasting over $20 billion in food yearly
Process
We began by figuring out our name. We wanted something that would relate to others and that would stick.

Then we addressed the problem, Second Nature transforms the act of buying into a meaningful partnership between communities. Instead of fleeting transactions, our platform builds lasting bonds by focusing on hyper-local exchanges. While others profit, we prioritise sustainability.
Solution
Rethink consumerism by encouraging borrowing, renting, and sharing within a community
Key Takeaway
Sustainability becomes more impactful when it's rooted in community, not just individual action. Giving users a platform to get help and give help makes it easier for everyone.
